Section 1

What is Quantum Fintech?

Quantum fintech applies quantum computing principles to solve financial problems that are intractable for classical computers.

Core idea

Classical computers use bits (0 or 1). Quantum computers use qubits that can exist in superposition, entanglement, and interference — giving exponential computational power for certain problems.

Finance is the perfect use case for quantum computing because it is full of NP-hard optimization problems, massive Monte Carlo simulations, and cryptographic challenges.

Classical limitation

Portfolio optimization (Markowitz model) for 100+ assets requires solving quadratic problems that explode in time complexity.

Quantum advantage

Quantum algorithms can explore 2ⁿ possibilities simultaneously. A 127-qubit machine can theoretically evaluate more portfolio combinations than there are atoms in the observable universe.

We are currently in the NISQ era (Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum) — machines with 50–1000 noisy qubits. But hybrid quantum-classical algorithms already deliver measurable advantages for real financial workloads.

Section 2 • Deep Dive

Quantum Computing Fundamentals for Fintech

1. Qubits & Superposition

A qubit can be in state \( |\psi\rangle = \alpha|0\rangle + \beta|1\rangle \) where \( |\alpha|^2 + |\beta|^2 = 1 \). Measuring collapses it to 0 or 1, but until then it represents all possibilities at once.

2. Entanglement

Two qubits can be correlated such that measuring one instantly determines the other — even across the universe. This is the basis of quantum parallelism in finance (e.g., pricing thousands of correlated derivatives simultaneously).

3. Key Algorithms in Fintech

QAOA (Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm)
Solves combinatorial optimization (portfolio selection, asset allocation, risk parity) by encoding problems as QUBO (Quadratic Unconstrained Binary Optimization) and using variational quantum circuits.
VQE (Variational Quantum Eigensolver)
Finds ground-state energy of Hamiltonian — used for quantum-enhanced Monte Carlo and option pricing under stochastic volatility models.
Quantum Machine Learning (QSVM, QNN)
Quantum kernels can detect subtle fraud patterns in high-dimensional transaction graphs that classical SVMs miss.
Shor’s Algorithm & Post-Quantum Crypto
Quantum computers break RSA/ECC. Quantum fintech also builds quantum-safe blockchain and Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) for secure inter-bank transfers.

Mathematical Example: Portfolio Optimization as QUBO

The Markowitz problem can be rewritten as:

Minimize: \( \mathbf{x}^T \Sigma \mathbf{x} - \mu^T \mathbf{x} + \lambda \mathbf{x}^T \mathbf{x} \)
Subject to: \( \sum x_i = 1 \), \( x_i \in \{0,1\} \)

This is converted to a Hamiltonian \( H = \sum_{i,j} Q_{ij} z_i z_j \) where qubits \( z_i \) represent asset inclusion.

A small team can solve the above on 20–50 qubits today with real advantage over classical solvers for 30+ asset portfolios.
